Coronavirus and Its origin

COVID-19 is the name given by WHO on February 11, 2020 which is the acronym of “Corona Virus Disease -2019” which is caused by a member of CoV family (coronavirus family). Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus-2 (SARS- CoV-2) is the name given to the 2019 novel coronavirus. COVID-19 is the name given to the disease associated with the virus.


SARS-CoV-2 is a new strain of coronavirus that has not been previously identified in humans.


Do You Know?
Coronavirus is closely related genetically to the SARS-CoV-1 virus. SARS emerged at the end of 2002 in China, and it caused more than 8 000 cases in 33 countries over a period of eight months. Around one in ten of the people who developed SARS died.

Coronaviruses are viruses that circulate among animals with some of them also known to infect humans. On 31 December 2019, a cluster of pneumonia cases of unknown etiology was reported in Wuhan, Hubei Province, China. On 9 January 2020, China CDC reported a novel coronavirus as the causative agent of this outbreak, coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19).

Because the first cases of the COVID-19 disease were linked to direct exposure to the Huanan Seafood Wholesale Market of Wuhan, the animal-to-human transmission was presumed as the main mechanism. Nevertheless, subsequent cases were not associated with this exposure mechanism. Therefore, it was concluded that the virus could also be transmitted from human-to-human, and symptomatic people are the most frequent source of COVID-19 spread.


Transmission and the Infection

While animals are believed to be the original source, the virus spread is now from human-to-human transmission. There is not enough epidemiological information currently to determine how easily this virus spreads between people. The virus seems to be transmitted mainly via small respiratory droplets (particles >5-10 μm in diameter) through sneezing, coughing, or when people interact with each other for some time in close proximity (usually less than one meter). These droplets can then be inhaled, or they can land on surfaces that others may encounter, who can then get infected when they touch their nose, mouth, or eyes. The virus can survive on different surfaces from several hours (copper, cardboard) up to a few days (plastic and stainless steel). However, the amount of viable virus declines over time and may not always be present in sufficient numbers to cause infection.

Do You Know?
The virus enters the host cell when the spike protein binds with the cellular receptor. Fluorescent studies confirmed that this cellular receptor is ACE2 (angiotensin-converting enzyme 2). A mutation N501T in the spike proteins is credited for enhanced binding to receptors and is the main reason for the quick spread of the virus.

The infectious period may begin one to two days before symptoms appear, but people are likely most infectious during the symptomatic period, even if symptoms are mild and very non-specific. The infectious period is now estimated to last for 7- 12 days in moderate cases and up to two weeks on average in severe cases.

Preliminary data from the EU/EEA (from the countries with available data) show that around 20-30% of diagnosed COVID-19 cases are hospitalized and 4% have severe illness. Hospitalization rates are higher for those aged 60 years and above, and for those with other underlying health conditions.

Protecting yourself and others from the spread COVID-19

If we, the citizens of this country take it as our responsibility to keep the virus spread in check then the present situation can be a lot better. For protection against this virus make sure you follow these guidelines.

Social Distancing: Maintaining a distance of at least 1m from people even at home.
Here is the reason:

The human to human spread of the virus is because of the small droplets released while talking, coughing, and sneezing. Therefore, a small distance will save you from encountering these droplets.

Using Alcohol based sanitizers: Frequently cleaning hand and surfaces of common contact with an alcohol-based sanitizer is good practice.
Here is the reason:
The alcohol will disrupt the coating of the virus and once its coating is destroyed the virus particles are neutralized.

Wearing a mask: Always wear a mask whenever you go out. Make sure the mask fits you and there is no problem in breathing.
Here is the reason:
Masks forms a protective layer around your nose and mouth. It holds all the droplets which are released whenever you speak, cough, and sneeze. Thus, you protect your dear ones.

Avoid touching your eyes, nose, ears, and mouth with your fingers.
Fingers might have encountered an infected surface and thus touching face will give an entry point to the virus into your body.

Do not Panic, Awareness is the Tonic!


With proper measures and awareness we can beat this virus and make our dear ones safe.

COVID-19 is caused by a Virus, NOT by bacteria: Coronavirus is the reason for the disease and therefore, use of anti-biotics is not recommended. Some people with Covid-19 might develop bacterial infection and in that case this medication can be helpful. Remember to consult a Healthcare personal always.

Wearing a medical mask will never cause respiratory problems: You may hear from people that wearing medical mask for prolonged period will lead to oxygen deficiency and difficulty in breathing. But this is completely irrelevant. You just have to make sure that the mask is worn properly and fits you well.


Do not panic from Covid-19: Most people who get Covid-19 recover from it. If you have a cough, fever and difficulty breathing call your health facility by telephone first. If you have fever and live in area with malaria or dengue seek medical care immediately. Not all fever is Covid-19.

A thermal scanner cannot detect covid-19: A fever may be caused by various reasons and will lead to rise in body temperature. A thermal scanner will just detect people with higher body temperature. Call your healthcare services for assistance or if you live in area with malaria or dengue.
